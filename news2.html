Valentino Will Show Its Men’s and Women’s Spring 2021 Collections in Milan

Headquartered in Rome and epitomizing la dolce vita, Valentino has shown its ready-to-wear, couture, and menswear collections in Paris for the better part of a decade. But in #timeslikethese, that routine two-hour flight poses significant hurdles, namely for members of its atelier. They’d have to fly, stay in hotels, and gather backstage at the show. Masks help, but COVID-19 cases are on the rise again in Europe, and any sort of travel comes with risks.
So Valentino is staying closer to home this season: On September 27, Pierpaolo Piccioli will present a coed spring 2021 show in Milan. In a release, he said: “I believe that, in this moment in time, it is of paramount importance to stay grounded and focused.... Milano is a new opportunity, a great project that I am developing with my teams with the aim of working around the idea of identity.”
“In the current scenario, we need to make decisions in advance to protect our first resources, our people, and to allow our brand to remain relevant exploring alternative opportunities,” chief executive officer Jacopo Venturini adds. “This is why we feel that it would be more ethical to produce the new show in Italy, in Milano. We strongly believe that this project will contribute to underline the importance of being part of a fashion system represented by both the Camera Nazionale della Moda Italiana and the Fédération de la Haute Couture et de la Mode.”
With several headliners absent from the Milan calendar, including Gucci and Bottega Veneta, Valentino will lend a boost of star power to the lineup. And while it isn’t explicitly noted in the release, taking the collection to Milan—a quick journey by train—will also make for a more environmentally friendly show. It isn’t just the logistics and health risks that challenge the need for fashion weeks; even before the pandemic, the constant air travel, lavish runway sets, and shipping felt wasteful in light of our climate crisis. Transportation is one of the top contributors to fashion’s carbon footprint, and if a designer can avoid shipping their collection all over the world, they should consider it. Gabriela Hearst had that in mind when she chose to show in Paris on October 4: Her samples are made in Italy, and shipping them a few hours north—rather than across the Atlantic to New York—felt right for this moment.
Beyond the new date, city, and coed lineup, Valentino didn’t share any details about what we can expect from the show. Check back in a couple of weeks when Vogue Runway begins covering the spring 2021 shows—physical, digital, phygital—all month long.
